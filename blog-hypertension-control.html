High Blood Pressure: Signs, Risks & How to Control It

Hypertension • 5 min read • Cardiac Evaluation

High blood pressure (hypertension) is one of the most common health problems in adults—and one of the easiest to miss. Many people feel completely normal while their blood pressure silently damages the heart, kidneys, eyes, and blood vessels.

Why hypertension is dangerous

  • Increases risk of stroke and heart attack
  • Can cause heart failure over time
  • Damages kidneys and accelerates kidney disease
  • Can affect vision and blood circulation

Common symptoms (and the truth)

Most people have no symptoms. Sometimes you may notice headaches, dizziness, or blurred vision—but don’t rely on symptoms. The only way to know is to check your blood pressure.

How to control blood pressure (what works)

  • Measure correctly: rest 5 minutes, sit upright, arm supported, avoid caffeine before checking.
  • Salt reduction: reduce processed foods, salty snacks, and too much added salt.
  • Activity: regular walking and light exercise improves BP control.
  • Weight management: even small changes can make a difference.
  • Medication consistency: taking your BP meds as prescribed is critical.

When you should see a doctor

  • BP consistently above normal on home checks
  • Headaches + very high readings
  • Chest pain, shortness of breath, fainting (seek urgent care)
  • Existing diabetes, kidney disease, or heart disease
