Chest Tightness, Wheezing or Cough: When to Get Checked

Chest / Asthma • 5 min read • Specialist Consultation

A cough, chest tightness, or wheezing can be caused by many things—flu, allergies, asthma, acid reflux, or even heart-related conditions. The key is recognising when symptoms are recurring, worsening, or affecting your daily life.

Common signs you should not ignore

  • Wheezing (a whistling sound when breathing)
  • Chest tightness that comes and goes
  • Cough that lasts longer than 2–3 weeks
  • Shortness of breath on mild activity
  • Night-time symptoms that wake you up

Triggers that can worsen symptoms

  • Dust, cold air, perfumes, smoke
  • Flu/infections and seasonal allergies
  • Physical exertion without a plan
  • Uncontrolled acid reflux (heartburn)

How a chest/asthma review helps

A proper consultation checks your symptom pattern, triggers, and response to medications. We also check for other causes that look like asthma and create a plan you can follow.

Seek urgent care if you have severe shortness of breath, fainting, blue lips, confusion, or chest pain that is intense or persistent.
