Heart Failure Basics: Symptoms, Triggers, and Why Follow-up Matters

Heart Failure • 6 min read • Cardiac Evaluation

Heart failure doesn’t mean the heart “stops.” It means the heart is not pumping efficiently enough to meet the body’s needs. With proper monitoring and treatment, many patients live well and stay stable.

Common symptoms

  • Shortness of breath (especially on walking or lying flat)
  • Swollen feet/ankles
  • Fatigue and reduced exercise tolerance
  • Weight gain from fluid retention
  • Persistent cough (sometimes at night)

What can worsen heart failure (triggers)

  • Missing medication doses
  • High salt intake
  • Uncontrolled blood pressure
  • Infections (flu, pneumonia)
  • Alcohol excess and poor sleep

Why follow-up is critical

Follow-ups allow medication adjustment, monitoring of symptoms, and prevention of hospital admissions. Patients often do best when they track symptoms early instead of waiting until severe.

Seek urgent care if breathing becomes very difficult, chest pain is severe, you faint, or swelling worsens rapidly.
