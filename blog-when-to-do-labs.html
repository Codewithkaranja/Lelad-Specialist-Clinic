When Should You Get Lab Tests? A Simple Guide for Adults

Laboratory • 6 min read • Laboratory Services

Lab tests help confirm a diagnosis, monitor chronic disease, and guide treatment changes. The right test at the right time can prevent complications and save you unnecessary worry.

When lab tests are useful

  • Persistent fatigue, weight changes, or loss of appetite
  • Monitoring diabetes, blood pressure, kidney conditions
  • Before starting or changing certain medications
  • Unexplained swelling, shortness of breath, or recurrent infections

Common tests doctors request (and why)

  • Blood sugar tests: diabetes diagnosis & monitoring
  • Kidney profile: checks filtration and hydration balance
  • Lipid profile: cholesterol risk assessment
  • Full blood count: anemia and infection indicators
  • Urine tests: protein, infection, kidney warning signs

How to prepare (simple)

  • Ask if you need fasting (some tests do)
  • Carry your medication list
  • Drink water unless instructed otherwise
  • Do not self-prescribe antibiotics before testing
