Understanding ECG & Echo: What These Heart Tests Actually Show

Diagnostics • 7 min read • Cardiac Evaluation

ECG and Echo are common heart tests, but they measure different things. Understanding them helps you know why your doctor recommends one (or both).

ECG (Electrocardiogram)

Checks the heart’s electrical activity and rhythm.

Echo (Echocardiogram)

Uses ultrasound to view the heart’s structure and pumping function.

When an ECG is useful

  • Chest pain or palpitations
  • Fast/slow heartbeat or irregular rhythm
  • Monitoring heart effects of blood pressure
  • Some medication and electrolyte issues

When an Echo is useful

  • Suspected heart failure
  • High blood pressure with symptoms or complications
  • Murmurs, valve issues, swelling, shortness of breath
  • Evaluating heart structure and ejection performance

Do these tests mean you have heart disease?

Not always. Often, they help rule out serious issues or guide the best next step. Results are interpreted alongside your symptoms, examination, and history.
